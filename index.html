Agenda Glossary

Key terms you’ll encounter in each committee’s background and resolutions.

Verbs

Co-design
To collaboratively design a product, service, or policy—involving end-users (e.g., youth, communities) directly in the creation process.
“WHO will co-design apps with adolescents offering screening tools and crisis chat lines.”
Convert
To change the function or use of an existing facility or asset.
“Convert libraries and community centers into ICT hubs offering structured after-school digital literacy programs and parental engagement sessions.”
Curate
To select, organize, and present resources or content with expert care, ensuring relevance and quality.
“UNESCO will curate open educational resources aligned with national standards.”
Deploy
To position or activate resources strategically (e.g., personnel, technology) in an operational context.
“Deploy remote sensing and on-site sensors to track ecological indicators.”
Embed
To integrate an element deeply within a system or institution.
“Embed trained counselors in schools with defined student-to-counselor ratios.”
Establish
To found or institute on a firm basis.
“Establish local cooperatives to manage tours, accommodation, and interpretation centers.”
Foster
To encourage or promote the development of something.
“Public–private partnerships can foster solar-powered classrooms.”
Harmonize
To align rules or standards across jurisdictions or organizations.
“Harmonize national legislation with the Arms Trade Treaty (ATT).”
Implement
To put into effect according to a plan or policy.
“Implement timed-entry systems and caps based on carrying-capacity studies.”
Incentivize
To motivate or encourage through incentives or rewards.
“Tax breaks can incentivize low-impact materials and renewable-energy installations.”
Monitor
To systematically observe and check progress or quality over time.
“Monitor corridor usage with ceasefire observers in real time.”
Negotiate
To discuss terms and reach mutually acceptable agreements.
“Negotiate a new Strategic Compass delineating EU and NATO roles.”
Pool
To combine resources into a joint mechanism for greater efficiency.
“Pool procurement for air-refueling and strategic transport assets.”
Reassess
To evaluate again with new information or changing circumstances.
“Reassess NATO’s strategic autonomy amid fluctuating U.S. security assurances.”

Nouns & Noun Phrases

Accelerator Networks
Programs or organizations that mentor and provide resources (e.g., funding, prototyping) to early-stage startups.
“Incubators and accelerator networks facilitate prototyping and investor-pitch events.”
Air-Refueling
The capability for aircraft to receive fuel mid-flight, extending operational range.
“Joint procurement of air-refueling assets can lower costs and enhance readiness.”
Arms Trade Treaty (ATT)
A global agreement to regulate the international trade in conventional arms.
“Promote universal ratification and implementation of the ATT.”
Ballistic Intelligence
Data and analysis used to trace the origin and movement of ballistic weapons.
“Regional tracking centers share ballistic intelligence to intercept illicit arms.”
Buffer Zone
A transitional area around a core site that limits human activity to protect sensitive resources.
“Create buffer zones with graduated access levels around heritage sites.”
Carrying Capacity
The maximum sustainable number of visitors or users without causing degradation.
“Caps based on scientifically assessed carrying capacities ensure site preservation.”
Community Tech Hub
A local center providing ICT access, training, and support to community members.
“Convert libraries into community tech hubs offering digital-literacy sessions.”
Digital Micro-Stamping
Embedding unique digital identifiers on weapons for forensic tracing.
“Customs officials use handheld readers to decode micro-stamps.”
Digital Pedagogy
Teaching methodologies optimized for digital or blended learning environments.
“Implement UNESCO-certified ‘Digital Pedagogy’ courses for educators.”
E-Learning Platform
A web-based system enabling remote or blended learning with interactive tools.
“Fund satellite-based e-learning platforms in remote regions.”
Green Infrastructure
Renewable or low-impact construction and technology solutions that reduce environmental footprints.
“Incentivize green infrastructure through tax breaks and micro-grants.”
Humanitarian Corridor
A safe passage designated for the delivery of aid or evacuation of civilians in conflict zones.
“Establish UN-monitored humanitarian corridors for medical evacuation.”
Innovation Sprint
A focused, time-bound period of collaboration aimed at rapid prototyping and problem-solving.
“Facilitate cross-border innovation sprints to stimulate green-tech collaboration.”
Open Educational Resource
Freely licensed teaching and learning materials that can be reused and adapted.
“Curate and localize OER curricula aligned with national standards.”
Peer-Support Network
A group in which members provide mutual emotional and practical support.
“Launch peer-support networks supervised by trained professionals in schools.”
Psychoeducation Module
An educational unit designed to teach psychological concepts and coping strategies.
“A psychoeducation module includes CBT-based worksheets and video tutorials.”
Regulatory Sandbox
A controlled environment where new products or services can be tested under relaxed regulations.
“Create a time-bound regulatory sandbox with compliance oversight.”
Remote Sensing
The acquisition of information about an object or area from a distance, typically via satellites or drones.
“Use multispectral remote sensing to detect deforestation hotspots.”
Strategic Autonomy
The ability to plan, decide, and act without reliance on external allies.
“Reassessing NATO’s strategic autonomy amid U.S. unpredictability.”
Strategic Compass
A framework that delineates roles and responsibilities for coordinated action.
“Negotiate a Strategic Compass to clarify EU and NATO roles.”
Strategic Sealift
Maritime capability for transporting large or heavy cargo over long distances.
“Charter roll-on/roll-off vessels to enhance strategic sealift capacity.”
Timed-Entry System
A booking mechanism that assigns visitors specific time slots to control crowds.
“Timed-entry systems cut peak-hour queues at heritage sites.”
Youth Ambassador
A young representative chosen to convey peer perspectives and lead outreach.
“Local youth ambassadors help destigmatize mental-health care.”

Adjectives

Age-Appropriate
Content or approaches suited in complexity and focus to a specific developmental group.
“Integrate age-appropriate consent modules into secondary-school curricula.”
Community-Led
Initiatives driven and managed by local community members to ensure ownership.
“Community-led stewardship ensures authenticity in heritage conservation.”
Low-Impact
Causing minimal disturbance to the environment or community.
“Specify low-impact lighting and materials in infrastructure plans.”
Sustainable
Meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet theirs.
“Adopt sustainable-mobility plans around urban heritage sites.”
Time-Bound
Having explicit deadlines or defined duration.
“The regulatory sandbox operates on a time-bound pilot of six months.”
Youth-Centric
Designed around the needs, preferences, and participation of young people.
“Develop youth-centric blended-finance instruments for green startups.”

Adverbs

Closely
With careful or detailed attention and coordination.
“Regulators will monitor sandbox progress closely to identify risks.”
Real-Time
Occurring instantaneously as events unfold, with minimal delay.
“Real-time analytics of footfall patterns inform on-the-fly adjustments.”
Universally
Applying across all relevant parties or contexts without exception.
“Aim for universally ratified standards in arms-control agreements.”
